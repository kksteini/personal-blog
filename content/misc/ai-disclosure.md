## TLDR

No AI was used to generate thoughts or research on the material found
in this blog. It was however used for refactoring the blog itself
and occasional proofreading, without altering the voice.

If you care, you can read the ADHD-fuelled-oversharing-dump below.

## My stance

I am not completely against AI as a tool
but I still strive to use it as little as possible.

For pedagogical texts and blogs,
I feel it is honest and preferable to struggle through words
and sentences like a horse on a bike. It is more human.

Whenever I read a blog or browse open source
projects, I check for AGENTS.md, and other tells in the
.gitignore, and commit messages.

I am suspicious of vibe coded projects and approach them
with more caution than otherwise human made content.
Maybe this sentiment will change in 5-10 years time but that's
not the point of this document, really.

I am not looking for a debate on this matter, I think the internet
has more than enough for you if that's what you wish to engage in.

For others, like myself, I include this document to be transparent
on what is human made and what AI has touched.

I wish more projects had an AI disclaimer.

## Zola theme refactor

When I started this blog I had a very bare bones layout.
Design, colour and theming are not my forte.
After I had written quite a lot of nonsense I wanted to
use a theme instead.

I tried to wrangle themes into the structure but couldn't get it to work
correctly.

**I got an AI agent to assist me in rewriting the structure and that helped me make
some headway. I'd say the endeavour was 70-80% AI and the rest human fixing.**

## Grammar and typos

I write everything myself. No sentence has been AI generated.

English is not my native language, but I think it's safe for me to think of it
as my clumsy second language. In some moments of self doubt
**I've had AI review my text** and asked it something along the lines of

```plain
Review this text for grammar mistakes and typos. 
Do not alter the voice of the author.
```

and then fixed it myself if I agree.
For comparison and fairness I've opted to not review this
document for grammar mistakes and typos.

Sometimes they have been AI proofread, though most often rejected if not
a grammar mistake or a typo.

*For a little while, a series heading had an AI generated description
after the Zola refactor which slipped my notice.
I replaced it with my own writing when I wrote
this document.*

## Researching

### Uiua

I do not use AI for researching Uiua. I don't think there's enough material
available to be devoured by the LLMs anyway.

What I do is read over the documentation again, search through the Discord,
asking my go-to nerd or asking a question in the **learning** channel of the
Discord.

### What about the Euler problems

I like struggling. I believe that learning requires struggle, at least for people
as rat brained as I am.
I am lucky to have a very smart group of friends that I can ask about maths
and computer science whenever the fancy takes me.

I don't get the same joy researching with AI as I do with a discussion.
So, for this blog I have not used AI for research.

Also, some Euler problems give you a solution guide upon completing it.
If I barely make it through with brute-forcing, I can read that guide as
well as the problem's discussion thread.

Finally, I really like this statement from project Euler's GenAI policy
> But if the genAI gives you the answer, you have gained nothing more than a
> badge of shame as you submit the answer to a problem you never solved.
